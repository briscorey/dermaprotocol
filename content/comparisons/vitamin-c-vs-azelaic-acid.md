<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Overview</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>Both vitamin C and azelaic acid are well-supported options for improving skin tone and addressing pigmentation. They work through different mechanisms and suit different skin types, so the better choice depends on your specific situation.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section" style="margin-top:var(--sp-10)">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Recommended Choice by Scenario</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">

<div class="alert alert--tip" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">If your main goal is brightening and sun protection</div>
Vitamin C is likely the better starting point. Use it in the morning under sunscreen for combined antioxidant and photoprotective benefits.
</div>

<div class="alert alert--tip" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">If you have acne and pigmentation together</div>
Azelaic acid addresses both concerns simultaneously with lower irritation risk. This makes it a strong first choice for combination acne-pigmentation issues.
</div>

<div class="alert alert--tip" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">If you have sensitive skin</div>
Start with azelaic acid. It is generally better tolerated than L-ascorbic acid serums, which require low pH formulations that can sting reactive skin.
</div>

<div class="alert alert--info">
<div class="alert__title">Can you use both?</div>
Yes. Many people use vitamin C in the morning (for antioxidant protection) and azelaic acid in the evening. This is a well-tolerated combination for most skin types.
</div>
