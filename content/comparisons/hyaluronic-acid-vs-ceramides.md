<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Overview</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>Hyaluronic acid and ceramides are both "hydration" ingredients, but they serve fundamentally different functions. Hyaluronic acid is a humectant — it draws water into the skin. Ceramides are barrier lipids — they prevent water from leaving. Understanding this difference is key to building an effective moisturising routine.</p>
</div>

<h2 class="detail-section__title">Recommended Choice by Scenario</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">

<div class="alert alert--tip" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">If your skin feels tight and dry despite moisturising</div>
You likely need <a href="/ingredients/ceramides/">ceramides</a>. The issue is probably barrier damage (water escaping) rather than insufficient water (which HA would address). A ceramide-rich moisturiser repairs the leak.
</div>

<div class="alert alert--tip" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">If your skin looks dull and lacks plumpness</div>
<a href="/ingredients/hyaluronic-acid/">Hyaluronic acid</a> serum applied to damp skin provides immediate hydration boost and plumping. Follow with a moisturiser to seal it in.
</div>

<div class="alert alert--tip" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">If you are recovering from over-exfoliation or retinoid irritation</div>
Ceramides are the priority — your barrier is the problem. See our <a href="/protocols/barrier-repair/">Barrier Repair Protocol</a>. HA can be layered underneath for extra hydration, but ceramides are the structural fix.
</div>

<div class="alert alert--info">
<div class="alert__title">Do you need both?</div>
For most people, yes. The optimal approach is a hyaluronic acid serum (applied to damp skin) sealed with a ceramide-containing moisturiser. This attracts water into the skin AND prevents it from leaving. Many well-formulated moisturisers contain both — check the ingredient list for both HA and ceramides. Add <a href="/supplements/omega-3/">oral omega-3s</a> for inside-out barrier lipid support.
</div>
