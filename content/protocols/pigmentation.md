<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Who This Is For</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>This protocol targets post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH), mild sun damage spots, and general uneven skin tone. Deep melasma or hormonal pigmentation often requires professional management.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Optional Upgrades</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<ul>
<li><strong>Retinol 0.3%</strong> — Add 2-3 nights per week (alternate with azelaic acid) after establishing tolerance</li>
<li><strong>Niacinamide 4-5%</strong> — Can be layered AM or PM for additional tone-evening support</li>
<li><strong>Alpha arbutin</strong> — A gentle alternative brightening agent if vitamin C is poorly tolerated</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Realistic Timeline</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>Pigmentation is slow to change. Expect 8-16+ weeks for visible improvement. Some deeper pigmentation takes 6+ months. Sunscreen consistency is the single biggest factor in preventing new pigmentation and protecting the work your actives are doing.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="alert alert--caution" style="margin-top:var(--sp-8)">
<div class="alert__title">Important</div>
Sunscreen is not optional in a pigmentation protocol. Without consistent, adequate sun protection, brightening actives will have limited impact, and pigmentation may worsen.
</div>
