<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Who This Is For</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>This protocol is for adults (typically 25+) who want to slow visible skin ageing, improve existing fine lines and texture, and maintain skin health long-term. It integrates topical skincare, oral supplements, and nutritional guidance — because skin ageing is driven by both external factors (UV, pollution) and internal factors (collagen decline, inflammation, oxidative stress).</p>
<p>Most anti-ageing advice only covers topicals. This protocol covers all three vectors.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Understanding Skin Ageing</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">

<div class="alert alert--info" style="margin-bottom:var(--sp-4)">
<div class="alert__title">The two types of ageing</div>
<strong>Extrinsic ageing (photoageing):</strong> Caused by UV radiation, pollution, and environmental damage. Responsible for up to 80% of visible facial ageing. Preventable.<br><br>
<strong>Intrinsic ageing:</strong> Genetically determined decline in collagen production (~1% per year after age 20), elastin degradation, reduced ceramide production, slower cell turnover. Partly manageable.
</div>

<p>The highest-impact intervention is UV protection (preventing extrinsic ageing). Everything else is secondary.</p>
</div>

<h2 class="detail-section__title">Nutritional Foundations</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>No supplement stack replaces a poor diet. The following nutritional factors have the strongest evidence for skin health:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Protein adequacy</strong> — Collagen is a protein. Insufficient total protein intake limits collagen synthesis regardless of supplementation. Aim for 1.2–1.6g/kg body weight daily.</li>
<li><strong>Antioxidant-rich foods</strong> — Colourful fruits and vegetables (berries, citrus, leafy greens, tomatoes) provide systemic antioxidant support. Lycopene from cooked tomatoes has some evidence for mild photoprotection.</li>
<li><strong>Omega-3 rich foods</strong> — Fatty fish (salmon, mackerel, sardines) 2–3 times weekly. Reduces systemic inflammation.</li>
<li><strong>Minimise high-glycaemic foods</strong> — High sugar intake accelerates glycation (cross-linking of collagen fibres), contributing to skin stiffness and wrinkle formation. This is moderate-quality evidence but biologically plausible.</li>
<li><strong>Adequate hydration</strong> — Chronic dehydration reduces skin turgor. No need for excessive water intake, but consistent adequate hydration matters.</li>
<li><strong>Moderate alcohol consumption</strong> — Alcohol is a vasodilator and dehydrator that accelerates skin ageing markers. Reducing intake has visible skin benefits for many people.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Realistic Timeline</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p><strong>Weeks 1–4:</strong> Retinoid adjustment period. Dryness, peeling, and mild irritation are normal. Supplement effects are building but not yet visible.</p>
<p><strong>Weeks 4–8:</strong> Skin texture begins improving. Hydration from HA and ceramides stabilises. Collagen peptide and omega-3 effects starting to accumulate.</p>
<p><strong>Weeks 8–12:</strong> Noticeable improvement in texture, tone, and skin quality. Fine lines from dehydration improve. Retinoid tolerance established.</p>
<p><strong>Months 3–6:</strong> Meaningful improvements in fine lines and skin elasticity. Collagen remodelling from retinoid becomes visible. This is where the combined protocol shows real results.</p>
<p><strong>Months 6–12+:</strong> Continued cumulative improvement. Anti-ageing skincare is a long-term commitment — the best results come from years of consistent practice, not weeks of intensive treatment.</p>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Common Mistakes</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<ul>
<li>Expecting visible wrinkle reduction in 2 weeks — meaningful changes take months</li>
<li>Focusing on expensive serums while skipping sunscreen — nothing undoes UV damage while it is still happening</li>
<li>Over-complicating the routine with 10+ products</li>
<li>Neglecting internal factors (diet, sleep, supplements) while perfecting the topical routine</li>
<li>Starting retinoids and multiple actives simultaneously</li>
<li>Comparing skin to filtered photos and AI-edited images</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div class="alert alert--caution" style="margin-top:var(--sp-8)">
<div class="alert__title">Important perspective</div>
Skin ageing is a natural biological process, not a disease. The goal of an evidence-based protocol is to slow preventable damage and maintain skin health — not to eliminate ageing entirely. Set realistic expectations and focus on consistency over intensity.
