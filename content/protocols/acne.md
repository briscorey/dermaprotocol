<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Who This Is For</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p>This protocol is designed for people with mild to moderate acne (blackheads, whiteheads, and inflammatory spots) who want a structured starting point. Severe or cystic acne usually requires professional dermatological care.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Optional Upgrades</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<ul>
<li><strong>Salicylic acid (BHA) 2%</strong> — Use 2-3 times per week as a treatment or cleanser for blackhead-prone areas</li>
<li><strong>Benzoyl peroxide 2.5%</strong> — Short-contact therapy (apply for 5-10 minutes, rinse) for inflammatory spots</li>
<li><strong>Retinol 0.25%</strong> — Add 1-2 nights per week after 6-8 weeks if tolerating the core routine well</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Common Mistakes</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<ul>
<li>Over-cleansing or using harsh, stripping cleansers</li>
<li>Introducing multiple actives at the same time</li>
<li>Skipping moisturiser because of oily skin</li>
<li>Expecting clear skin in 2 weeks — most protocols need 6-12 weeks</li>
<li>Picking or extracting spots manually</li>
</ul>
</div>
</div>

<div class="detail-section">
<h2 class="detail-section__title">Realistic Timeline</h2>
<div class="detail-section__body">
<p><strong>Weeks 1-2:</strong> Adjustment period. Some purging may occur with actives like azelaic acid or retinol.</p>
<p><strong>Weeks 4-6:</strong> Reduction in new breakouts for many people. Inflammatory spots may begin to calm.</p>
<p><strong>Weeks 8-12:</strong> Clearer picture of whether the protocol is working. If no improvement, consider professional assessment.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="alert alert--caution" style="margin-top:var(--sp-8)">
<div class="alert__title">When to see a professional</div>
If your acne is severe, cystic, leaving scars, or not responding to OTC approaches after 12 weeks, consult a dermatologist. Prescription options like tretinoin, antibiotics, or isotretinoin may be appropriate.
</div>
